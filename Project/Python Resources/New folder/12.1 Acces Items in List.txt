<h2><span style="color: #000080;">Range of indexes:</span></h2>
<p>Python allows to access a range of items in a list by passing the range indexes.</p>
<p><strong>for example:</strong></p>
<table style="margin: auto; box-shadow: 3px 3px 10px #000;" border="1">
<tbody>
<tr style="background-color: rgba(0,0,0,0.1);">
<td>theList = [<span class="pythonstringcolor" style="color: #008000;">"Sam"</span>,<span class="pythonstringcolor" style="color: #008000;">"Sara"</span>,<span class="pythonstringcolor"><span style="color: #008000;">"John"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"Smith"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"Adam"</span></span>]<br /><span class="pythonkeywordcolor" style="color: #800000;">print</span>(theList [<span class="pythonnumbercolor"><span style="color: #800000;">1</span>:<span style="color: #800000;">3</span></span>])</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<p><strong>For example:</strong></p>
<table style="margin: auto; box-shadow: 3px 3px 10px #000;" border="1">
<tbody>
<tr style="background-color: rgba(0,0,0,0.1);">

</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
