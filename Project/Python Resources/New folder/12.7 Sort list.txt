<h1><span style="color: #000080;">Sort list:</span></h1>
<p>Python provides <span style="color: #800000;">sort()</span> method by default to sort list&nbsp;alphanumerically, ascending.</p>
<p><strong>For example:</strong></p>
<table style="margin: auto; box-shadow: 3px 3px 10px #000;" border="1">
<tbody>
<tr style="background-color: rgba(0,0,0,0.1);">
<td>
<p>thelist = [<span style="color: #008000;">"Toyota"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"Ford"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"Benz"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"Honda"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"BMW"</span>]</p>
<p>thelist.<span style="color: #800000;">sort</span>()</p>
<p><span style="color: #800000;">print</span>(thelist)</p>
<p><span style="color: #808080;"># Returns:['BMW','Benz','Ford','Honda','Toyota']</span></p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr style="background-color: rgba(0,0,0,0.1);">
<td>
<p>thelist = [<span style="color: #008000;">214</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">5</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">12</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">110</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">14</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">34</span>]</p>
<p>thelist.<span style="color: #800000;">sort</span>()</p>
<p><span style="color: #800000;">print</span>(thelist)</p>

</tr>

<h2><span style="color: #000080;">Descending sort:</span></h2>
<p>We can pass the reverse argument and set it to <span style="color: #800000;">True</span> to <span style="color: #800000;">sort()</span> method if we want a descending sorted list.</p>
<p><strong>For example:</strong></p>
<table style="margin: auto; box-shadow: 3px 3px 10px #000;" border="1">
<tbody>
<tr style="background-color: rgba(0,0,0,0.1);">
<td>
<p>thelist = [<span style="color: #008000;">"Toyota"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"Ford"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"Benz"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"Honda"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"BMW"</span>]</p>
<p>thelist.<span style="color: #800000;">sort</span>(<span style="color: #800000;">reverse</span> = True)</p>
<p><span style="color: #800000;">print</span>(thelist)</p>
<p><span style="color: #808080;"># Returns: ['Toyota','Honda','Ford','Benz','BMW']</span></p>
</td>
</tr>
<tr style="background-color: rgba(0,0,0,0.1);">
<td>
<p>thelist = [<span style="color: #008000;">214</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">5</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">12</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">110</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">14</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">34</span>]</p>
<p>thelist.<span style="color: #800000;">sort</span>(<span style="color: #800000;">reverse</span> = True)</p>
<p><span style="color: #800000;">print</span>(thelist)</p>
<p><span style="color: #808080;"># Returns: [214, 110, 34, 14, 12, 5]</span></p>
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<h2><span style="color: #000080;">Case sensitivity:</span></h2>
<p><span style="color: #800000;">sort()</span> method is case sensitive. Basically, it positions the uppercase letters before the lowercase letters.</p>
<p><strong>For example:</strong></p>
<table style="margin: auto; box-shadow: 3px 3px 10px #000;" border="1">
<tbody>
<tr style="background-color: rgba(0,0,0,0.1);">
<td>
<p>thelist = [<span style="color: #008000;">"c"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"d"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"b"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"C"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"B"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"a"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"A"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"D"</span>]</p>
<p>thelist.<span style="color: #800000;">sort</span>()</p>
<p><span style="color: #800000;">print</span>(thelist)</p>
<p><span style="color: #808080;"># Return:&nbsp;['A', 'B', 'C', 'D', 'a', 'b', 'c', 'd']</span></p>
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<h2><span style="color: #000080;">Case insensitive:</span></h2>
<p>We can pass a <span style="color: #800000;">keyword argument</span> to <span style="color: #800000;">sort()</span> method if returning lowercase letters before uppercase letters is the case.</p>
<p><strong>For example:</strong></p>
<table style="margin: auto; box-shadow: 3px 3px 10px #000;" border="1">
<tbody>
<tr style="background-color: rgba(0,0,0,0.1);">
<td>
<p>thelist = [<span style="color: #008000;">"c"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"d"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"b"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"C"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"B"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"a"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"A"</span>,<span style="color: #008000;">"D"</span>]</p>
<p>thelist.<span style="color: #800000;">sort</span>(<span style="color: #000000;">key </span>= <span style="color: #800000;">str</span>.<span style="color: #000000;">lower</span>)</p>
<p><span style="color: #800000;">print</span>(thelist)</p>
<p><span style="color: #808080;"># Return:&nbsp;['a', 'A', 'b', 'B', 'c', 'C', 'd', 'D']</span></p>
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<h2><span style="color: #000080;">Customizing sort function:</span></h2>
<p>We can have our own keyword argument and pass it to <span style="color: #800000;">sort()</span> function to have a customized output.</p>
<p><strong>For example:</strong></p>
<table style="margin: auto; box-shadow: 3px 3px 10px #000;" border="1">
<tbody>
<tr style="background-color: rgba(0,0,0,0.1);">
<td>
<p><span style="color: #800000;">def</span> <span style="color: #800000;">func</span>(a):<br />&nbsp; &nbsp;<span style="color: #800000;">return abs</span>(a - 0)</p>
<p>thelist = [<span style="color: #008000;">110</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">5</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">12</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">14</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">34</span>, <span style="color: #008000;">75</span>]</p>
<p>thelist.<span style="color: #800000;">sort</span>(key = <span style="color: #800000;">func</span>)</p>
<p><span style="color: #800000;">print</span>(thelist)</p>
<p><span style="color: #808080;"># Returns:&nbsp;[5, 12, 14, 34, 75, 110]</span></p>
</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p>The output will be a list of values based on how close they are to 0.</p>
